# Mr. James - Complete Beat Sheet

## Structural Overview
- **Genre:** Drama/Fantasy (Magical Realism)
- **Target Length:** 110 pages
- **Time Structure:** Memory Bridge (65% present / 35% flashback)
- **Thematic Core:** "Watch out for minos" - the labyrinths we build around our wounds

---

## ACT ONE: THE SETUP (pp. 1-25)

### Opening Image (p. 1)
Storage unit auction in Cleveland. Three strangers - MARCUS (scrubs, checking phone), DELIA (comedy club shirt, sunglasses), TERRENCE (army jacket, watchful) - stand apart. Unit 47 opens: childhood treasures from "THE SANCTUARY." All three freeze with recognition.

### Theme Stated (p. 5)
MRS. PATTERSON (Mr. James's sister) delivers envelope with map, key, and letter. States theme: "Watch out for minos. The labyrinth is always of your own making."

### Set-Up (pp. 1-10)
Three separate lives established:
- **Marcus:** Healthcare admin, can't accept help, exhausted
- **Delia:** Failed comedian, running from grief, no permanent home
- **Terrence:** VA counselor, emotionally closed, compulsive exercise

### Catalyst (p. 12)
At diner, they open envelope: map of Cleveland with renamed locations. Mr. James's letter: "The Sanctuary is not a place. You three ARE the Sanctuary." Marcus refuses call - hospital emergency.

### Debate (pp. 12-25)
Individual crises push them toward decision:
- Marcus collapses from exhaustion, forced to take leave
- Delia spirals, almost drinks, goes to AA meeting
- Terrence receives Mr. James's compass from Mrs. Patterson

**FLASHBACK #1 (pp. 18-22): "The First Summer"**
1999: Three 10-year-olds meet Mr. James at rec center. "Who here believes in magic? The ones who don't are usually the ones who need it most." Compass introduced.

### Break Into Two (p. 25)
They reunite at abandoned recreation center (The Sanctuary). Key opens lock. Inside: decay and preservation. Fresh paint on wall: "THE WORK CONTINUES. REMEMBER."

---

## ACT TWO-A: FUN AND GAMES (pp. 25-55)

### B Story (p. 30)
Meet HOPE (28), Mr. James's grandniece, maintaining the Sanctuary. "He said you were his greatest success and his greatest failure." Mystery of Mr. James begins.

### Fun and Games (pp. 30-55)

**Quest Stop #1: The Dragon's Gate (pp. 30-38)**
Old bridge at midnight. Hidden envelopes with photos and messages naming their dragons.

**FLASHBACK #2 (pp. 38-42): "The Dragon's Gate"**
1999: Night walk to bridge. Young Marcus withdrawn since father left. Mr. James: "The bravest thing a person can do is ask for help."

**Quest Stop #2: The Whispering Archive (pp. 42-48)**
Library archives reveal Mr. James's history: lost family in fire, hospitalized, founded summer program.

**FLASHBACK #3 (pp. 48-52): "The Whispering Archive"**
1999: Library quest. Young Delia asks why sad things happen. Mr. James: "Because the world is broken. And it stays broken unless people decide to fix it."

**Quest Stop #3: The Keeper's Garden (pp. 52-55)**
Cemetery. Mr. James's grave beside wife and daughter.

### Midpoint (p. 55)
Cemetery. Marcus breaks: "He spent his final months setting up a scavenger hunt for three people who abandoned him." Each confesses they weren't there when he died.

---

## ACT TWO-B: BAD GUYS CLOSE IN (pp. 55-75)

### The Fracture (pp. 55-60)
Old resentments surface. They separate.

### Individual Crises (pp. 60-65)
- Marcus collapses, must call 911 alone
- Delia almost drinks, shares about mother at AA
- Terrence PTSD breakdown in parking lot

**FLASHBACK #4 (pp. 65-70): "The Minos"**
1999: Hedge maze. "The minotaur wasn't the enemy. The labyrinth was." Red strings tied to wrists: "You three are each other's thread."

### Closing In (pp. 70-75)
Hope shares journal: "Forgive yourselves, children. I already have."

### All Is Lost (p. 75)
Final entry: "I'm scared. I don't want to die alone. I called Marcus today. He didn't answer."

### Dark Night of the Soul (pp. 75-85)
Confessions in darkness. Each reveals who they failed to be there for.

**FLASHBACK #5 (pp. 80-85): "The Thread"**
1999: Final summer day. Gifts given. "The work isn't magic. The work IS magic."

---

## ACT THREE: THE RESOLUTION (pp. 85-110)

### Break Into Three (p. 85)
Marcus asks for help: "I can't do this alone. I need you both."

### Finale (pp. 85-110)
- Learn Sanctuary left to them, building condemned
- Mobilize hundreds of former program kids
- **FLASHBACK #6 (pp. 100-103):** Mr. James's faith in them
- City council testimony, unanimous vote, Sanctuary saved

### Final Image (p. 110)
Six months later. New summer program. Marcus kneels before children:
"Who here believes in magic?"

Wall: **"THE WORK CONTINUES."**

**FADE OUT.**
