# /pitch-worksheet

Generate a structured pitch worksheet for your screenplay concept.

## Arguments
- `[title]` - Optional: The screenplay title

## Workflow

### Step 1: Identify Screenplay
If title not provided, ask:
```
Which screenplay are we developing a pitch for?

Enter the title or describe your concept:
```

### Step 2: Core Elements
Gather essential information:

```
Let's build your pitch worksheet.

LOGLINE - What is your story about? (1-2 sentences max)
>

GENRE - What genre? (stick to one, or note if mixing)
>

THE 3 MAIN CHARACTERS (including the antagonist):
1. Name and brief description:
2. Name and brief description:
3. Name and brief description:
```

### Step 3: Three-Act Summary
```
ACT 1 - Introduce your main character. How do they live?
What moment changes everything for them?
>

ACT 2 - Intensify the conflict. What stands in their way?
When are they furthest from their goal?
>

ACT 3 - How does it resolve? What's the ending?
>
```

### Step 4: Cinematic Elements
```
TURNING POINTS - 3 flashes that change the story direction:
1.
2.
3.

MOST CINEMATIC SCENES - The ones you can really visualize:
1.
2.
3.

B-STORY - Your secondary plot:
>
```

### Step 5: Generate Worksheet
Create formatted pitch worksheet document:

```markdown
# PITCH WORKSHEET: [TITLE]

## LOGLINE
[Refined logline]

## GENRE
[Genre(s)]

## MAIN CHARACTERS
1. **[Name]** - [Description]
2. **[Name]** - [Description]
3. **[Name]** - [Description]

## ACT ONE
[Summary]

## ACT TWO
[Summary]

## ACT THREE
[Summary]

## KEY TURNING POINTS
1. [Turning point]
2. [Turning point]
3. [Turning point]

## MOST CINEMATIC SCENES
1. [Scene description]
2. [Scene description]
3. [Scene description]

## B-STORY
[Secondary plot]

---
*Pitch Worksheet - Ready for verbal delivery*
```

### Step 6: Verbal Pitch Prep
```
Your pitch worksheet is ready!

Practice tip: Don't memorize - tell it like you're sharing
an exciting story with a friend.

Ready to practice your verbal pitch?
  Yes - I'll help you rehearse
  No  - I'll save the worksheet
```

## Success Criteria
- [ ] Logline is 1-2 sentences
- [ ] Genre clearly identified
- [ ] 3 main characters described
- [ ] All three acts summarized
- [ ] Turning points identified
- [ ] Cinematic moments captured
- [ ] Worksheet document created
